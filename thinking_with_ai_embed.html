Thinking with AI began not as a curriculum proposal but as an active inquiry — enrolling in existing programs, identifying their limits, and building a more rigorous alternative through sustained practice. The syllabus is itself a portfolio artifact, produced through the methods it teaches.

Eight weeks. Participants bring their own expertise — in qualitative inquiry, cultural analysis, place-based research, or any human-centered field — and engage AI as both tool and subject. No technical background assumed. The experience itself is the intellectual product.

The program holds five simultaneous identities — held in the diagram to the right — and what they share is the center: a simultaneous inquiry that doesn't resolve into any single one of them. Every element operates as both instrument and object of inquiry — the texts, the tools, the syllabus itself. What doesn't change is the framework: the questions, the methods, and the conviction that deep domain expertise, brought into rigorous AI collaboration, produces original intellectual work.

Each participant leaves with a Field Application Design document: a domain-specific framework for rigorous AI engagement, developed through the program and theirs to carry forward. Across participants, these documents accumulate into something that doesn't yet exist — a cross-disciplinary map of AI's relationship to human-centered practice.

The Field Application Design is a culmination of what each week's reading, practice, reflection, and collaboration produced.

This program is a working draft — evolving through the methods it teaches.
